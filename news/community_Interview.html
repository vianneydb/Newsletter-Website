Lack of women in STEM

March 21, 2024 by Vianney Diaz

SUBJECT: The principal obstacles that women face during pursuing a STEM career and how they faced it to success.

Facts and Data

The invisibility and lack of female models in STEM, specifically in engineering, means that fewer and fewer women want to enter the area of computing, in addition to having consequences on their studies and work lives.

Following the data from the fact sheet of the College of Engineering at UTEP. In the Fall of 2022, a year ago, the total enrollment of students in the College of Engineering was 4,416. Where 79% of the student body profile were men and only 21% of the students were women.

Given the same example in Fall 2022, only 22 women were part of the faculty profile out of the total faculty staff which was 116.

Interview - Women in STEM

Fernanda Zuany | Interview

She opens up a whole new perspective on women in the computer science field. In addition to her experience as a Computer Science student, she offers clear ideas and statements about the main obstacles that women must face, both in the society that makes them invisible and in the little support they receive in their environment.

She told us about the journey she had to go through, who inspired her to become president of the ACM-W WICS organization and how she impacted her life and the lives of other students she inspired.

"I think it is really important to have someone in front of the class that you feel like identified with and said like, Oh my god if she does it, I can do it too!"

- Zuany Fernanda, President of ACM-W WICS

It also offers a more assertive perspective on the vision of the organization, what its objectives are, and how this organization helped other students by encouraging and supporting them in their studies, offering them conferences, support groups, networking, and opportunities to expand their skills, and making women in STEM feel more self-comfortable and more assertive in their laboral experiences, which is mostly ruled by men.
