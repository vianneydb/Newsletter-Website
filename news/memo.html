Parking and Transportation Services to The University of Texas at El Paso

February 8, 2024 by Vianney Diaz

SUBJECT: Memo to Parking and Transportation Services UTEP, discussing about saturated public transportation issue for student community.

Time Problem

Almost 80% of the student body of the University of Texas at El Paso is made up of the Latino community. It is known that most of its students cross the Mexico-US border daily to get to their classesâ€”often from the Santa Fe Bridge, or Americas Bridge, and need to use the Sun Metro public transportation to arrive at the bus stop closest to UTEP.

Generally, during rush hours students need to board the bus around 7 or 8 a.m. to get on time for their classes since public transportation does not cover the demands of people who board the bus. Then, at 5 p.m., the buses going downtown are usually full because all high schools and most jobs have the same departure time, leaving UTEP students waiting up to one and a half hours to get on a bus.

Students who live in Mexico spend time waiting to board the bus, time that they could use for study or homework, directly affecting their academic development, mental health, and success. Being able to arrive or leave college on time shouldn't be a very stressful situation for students. However, the long line to take the bus shows the opposite, making students center their attention on arriving two or three hours before their class time, sacrificing sleep and study time. It also affects their class schedules preventing them from staying after 6 p.m., and demanding to run to the bus stop every day expecting to stay 1 extra hour to board the bus.

Santa Fe Bridge | El Heraldo de Juarez

Giving them options

The transportation option for UTEP students on behalf of the school is the Miner Metro Shuttle. This system circles the furthest parking locations in the UTEP area, giving the facility to students, faculty, and staff to arrive at different locations of the University following two established routes, but always following the perimeters of the school.

The Campus Loop route runs from Miner Alley to the Campbell Building at 1101 N. Campbell St. and will service Glory Road and Oregon St. stops. (Miner Metro, n.d.) But this route is still too far for students who cross every day from the most common bridges, or downtown.

Opening another route to downtown, near the Sun Metro station, could also help students who live far from the university take other routes that could be faster to reach their destinations without having to transfer.

The University of Texas at El Paso should provide students living in Juarez, Mexico with the opportunity to get to class on time using the same resources they have, the Minner Shuttle Metro, instead of the closest ones to the school, which are the routes 10, 14, brio 205 and the streetcar.
